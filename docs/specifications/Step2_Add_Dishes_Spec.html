1. High-level brief

What this page is for. The customer has already chosen a box size in Step 1. On this page they fill that box with dishes from the menu. They can search and filter the menu, look at a dish in detail, personalise it, add it, change how many they want, and remove it. A live "Your box" panel shows what they have chosen and what it will cost.

The one job of the page. Get the customer to fill every space in their box, then send them to Step 3.

Where it sits. Choose box (1) → Add dishes (2) → Extras (3) → Review (4) → Checkout (5).

The three things that make it non-trivial:

  • A box has a fixed number of spaces. The customer can never hold more dishes than the box holds. There is no way to buy a dish "on the side" — to add more, the box itself must get bigger. This is a deliberate decision point, not a silent auto-upgrade.
  • The same dish can appear in the box more than once with different personalisation. Each unique combination is its own line, and the card has to represent that clearly.
  • Price is built from three parts: the box price, personalisation surcharges, and signature upgrades. Box size is the only thing that changes how many dishes are paid for.
Brand rule that must not be broken. Individual dishes never show a standalone price. Only the box, add-ons, personalisation surcharges and signature upgrades carry prices. A dish card may show "+£4" as a signature upgrade, but never "£12" as a dish price.

Group names, option labels, option order, prices, which option is Abby's choice, and which groups exist at all are set in the back end and editable without a release. Important: the surcharge for a personalisation is the difference from Abby's choice, not the raw price of the option. Because Abby's side (Wild rice) already costs £2, choosing "No side" produces a negative £2 adjustment. Negative adjustments are legitimate and must be supported end to end.

Formula, per unit:

surcharge = sum over every option group of
              (chosen option price − that group's Abby's-choice price)

The formula must loop over whatever groups the back end returns, including heat and any group added later. Do not hard-code four groups. Where a group allows more than one option at once (flagged on the dish record), every selected option's price is added before the Abby's-choice value is subtracted once.

3.3 The 3 parts of the total

PartHow it is worked out
Box pricePrice for the current box size (see 3.1)
PersonalisationSurcharge × line quantity, summed across all lines. No base/extra split — every unit is a box unit.
Signature upgradesThe dish's upgrade amount × total quantity, for every signature dish
total = boxPrice + personalisation + signatureUpgrades
Why extras disappeared — and a finding from the prototype. The prototype carried a full extras pricing model: a base/extra split, £15 per extra unit, and a separate extra-personalisation row. It was unreachable. Every path that could push the box past its size — add from a card, increase from a card, increase from the box panel, add from the modal — already diverted into the grow-the-box flow, and shrinking the box below what was selected was already blocked. No customer could ever have produced an extra unit. Removing extras loses no behaviour; it deletes a second, contradictory pricing model that was sitting behind a guard.

FR-1 Header and progress Front end

FR-1.1
Show a 5-step progress indicator

  • Step 2 shows as the current step (number visible, emphasised).
  • Steps 3 to 5 show as not started.
  • Steps are display only. They are not clickable on this page.
FR-1.2
Show a compact progress indicator on small screens

  • Show a progress bar filled to 40% (step 2 of 5).
FR-1.3

  • The logo links to the homepage.
  • "Questions?" opens the help panel (FR-16).
FR-1.4
Back link

FR-2 Search Front end

Changed FR-2.1
Search the menu by text
  • Match the typed text against the dish name, the short description, the dietary labels, and a per-dish list of search keywords set in the back end.
  • Keywords are invisible to the customer. They exist so the team can catch spellings, regional names and cravings the dish text does not contain — "goat", "shaki", "rice dish", "comfort", "party" — without changing the copy on the card.
  • Keywords are editable per dish without a release.
  • Matching is case-insensitive and matches anywhere in the text, not just the start.
  • Results update as the customer types. No submit button is required.
  • Placeholder text: "Search dishes, proteins or goals" on desktop, "Search dishes" on mobile.
FR-2.2
Clear the search
  • A clear control appears once text has been entered.
  • Clearing resets the search text and resets the page back to the first 8 results.
FR-2.3
Searching resets paging
  • Any change to the search text resets the visible count back to 8.

FR-3 Filters Front end Back end

FR-3.1
Offer four filter groups
GroupOptions
CategoryMains, Soups & Stews, Sides, Proteins, Swallows, Snacks
DietaryVegetarian, Vegan, Protein rich, Gluten-free
Spice levelMild, Medium, Hot

The table above is the launch configuration.

Extended in v1.1 — v1.0 made the options configurable; the groups now are too.
  • The filter groups themselves are configuration, not code. The back end returns an ordered list of groups, each with its own name, icon, selection behaviour and option list.
  • The front end renders whatever it is given. Adding a fifth group, renaming a group, reordering options or retiring a group must need no front-end change.
  • Each group declares how it matches: by a field on the dish (category, dietary), by an exact value (spice), or by a numeric range (calories).
New FR-3.5
Range filters are defined in the back end
  • The calorie bands ("Under 500 kcal", "500+ kcal") are a range filter, not two fixed labels.
  • The back end supplies the label, the field it reads, and the lower and upper bounds.
  • The same mechanism must work for any future numeric band — a protein or salt filter, say — without new front-end code.
FR-3.2
How filters combine
  • Within one group, options are combined with OR. Picking Vegan and Vegetarian shows dishes that are either.
  • Between groups, filters are combined with AND. Picking Mains and Vegan shows vegan mains only.
  • Search text is combined with the filters using AND.
  • A group with nothing selected does not restrict anything.
FR-3.3
Open, close and apply
  • The filter panel opens and closes from a single control.
  • Filters apply immediately as they are picked. There is no separate Apply step on desktop.
  • On small screens the panel is a full sheet with a close control and an apply button labelled "Show N dishes", where N is the live result count.
  • Clicking outside the panel closes it.
FR-3.4
Show and remove active filters
  • Every selected filter appears as a removable chip in the result bar.
  • Removing a chip removes only that one filter.
  • "Clear all" clears the search text and every filter group, and resets paging to 8.
  • The chip row and "Clear all" only appear when at least one filter is active.

Changed FR-5.3
Show personalisation state — four cases

The card's personalisation block has four distinct states. v1.0 described two of them.

StateWhenWhat the card shows
A. Not in the box Dish has never been added "Personalise this dish" link, plus a sub-line naming the option groups actually available for this dish
B. In the box, Abby's choice One version, everything left at the default "Abby's choice" marker and an "Edit" link. Do not list the individual options — naming them implies the customer chose them.
C. In the box, personalised One version, at least one option changed "Personalised" marker, a one-line summary of what was chosen, and an "Edit" link
D. In the box, multiple versions Two or more versions of the same dish "N versions in your box" and an "Edit" link. No option summary — there is no single set of options to name.

Sub-line for state A

  • The sub-line names only the groups this dish offers.
  • A dish offering all four reads "Portion, protein, side & heat — your way."
  • A dish offering only portion and heat reads "Portion & heat — your way."
  • A dish offering nothing hides the personalise link entirely (FR-11.5).

Quantity shown on the card

  • In states B and C the stepper shows that version's quantity and acts on it directly.
  • In state D the number shown is the total units of that dish across all versions.
Open question — needs your decision. In state D, what should the card's plus and minus controls act on? There is no single version to target. Three options: (a) hide the stepper in state D and leave quantity changes to the box panel and the modal, where the version is unambiguous; (b) keep the stepper, and have it open the version picker rather than change anything directly; (c) keep the stepper, and have plus add another unit of the most recently added version. (a) is my recommendation — a control that silently picks a version on the customer's behalf is the kind of thing that produces a wrong delivery. But it is a product call, not a technical one.

FR-6 Adding, changing quantity and removing Front end

Already covered FR-6.1
Add a dish from a card
Your point 3 — this rule was already correct in v1.0 and is unchanged.
  • If the box has a free space, add one unit using Abby's choice personalisation and confirm with a toast.
  • If the box is full, do not add the dish. Open the "make box larger" flow instead (FR-9), remembering the dish so it is added automatically once the box has grown.
FR-6.2
Merge identical selections into one line
  • When adding a unit, look for an existing line with the same dish and exactly the same personalisation.
  • If one exists, increase its quantity by 1.
  • If none exists, create a new line with quantity 1.
  • Two personalisations match only when portion, protein set, side and heat are all the same. Protein order does not matter.
FR-6.3
Increase quantity
  • The plus control adds one more unit to that line.
  • If the box is already full, open the "make box larger" flow instead, remembering the intent.
  • From a dish card, the plus control targets the line that uses Abby's choice if one exists, otherwise the most recently created line for that dish.
FR-6.4
Decrease quantity
  • The minus control reduces the line quantity by 1.
  • When the quantity would reach 0, delete the line entirely.
  • From a dish card, the minus control targets the most recently created line for that dish.
FR-6.5
Remove a line
  • "Remove" deletes the whole line regardless of quantity.
  • Confirm with a toast: "Dish name removed".
  • No confirmation dialog. The action is easy to reverse by adding again.

Changed FR-10.2
Nutrition and ingredients come from the back end
v1.0 was wrong here. It said to multiply every figure by 2 for a Full table portion. The front end must never calculate nutrition.
  • Nutrition figures, ingredients and allergens are authored in the back end for every combination that changes them.
  • When the customer changes an option, the front end displays the figures the back end has supplied for that combination. It does not scale, multiply or derive anything.

  • The caption states which preparation the figures apply to, e.g. "Per serving — Light table 225g".

  • Dishes default to Abby's choice.
  • The customer opts in via "Personalise this dish" on the card or in the modal.
  • Opening a dish that is already personalised opens the personalisation panel already expanded and scrolls to it.
Changed FR-11.2
Option groups are configuration
  • The back end returns an ordered list of option groups. Portion, protein, side and heat are the launch set, not a fixed structure.
  • Each group carries its own label, help text, options, prices, Abby's choice, and whether it allows one selection or several.
  • Every option can carry a price, including heat options. The front end must not assume any group is free.
  • Labels and prices are editable without a release. "Light table" could become "Everyday portion", and 225g could become 250g, with no front-end change.
  • Abby's choice in each group is marked so the customer can see the recommendation.
  • Each option shows its price difference from Abby's choice, including negative differences.
New FR-11.5
Personalisation options vary per dish
  • Each dish declares which option groups apply to it, and which options within those groups are available.
  • A dish may offer all four groups, some of them, or none.
  • A dish may offer a group with a reduced option list — a fish dish that offers salmon and prawns but not chicken, for example.
  • A dish may set its own Abby's choice, different from the catalogue default.
  • Where a dish offers no personalisation at all, hide the "Personalise this dish" link on the card and the personalisation panel in the modal. Do not show an empty panel or a disabled link.
  • The card's sub-line names only the groups that dish offers (FR-5.3).
  • The back end must reject a personalisation containing an option the dish does not offer, even when that option exists in the global catalogue.
FR-11.3
Multi-protein dishes
  • Some dishes allow more than one protein. This is a property of the dish, set in the back end.
  • On those dishes, protein selection is multi-select and at least one must stay selected.
  • Every selected protein's surcharge is added.
FR-11.4
Summarise the personalisation
  • Build a short summary listing only what differs from Abby's choice, e.g. "Full table · Salmon · Medium heat".
  • If nothing differs, treat the dish as not personalised even if the customer opted in.

FR-12 Signature dishes Front end Back end

FR-12.1
Marking
  • A signature dish is marked on the card, in the modal and in the box list.
  • The upgrade amount is shown next to the marker, e.g. "+£4".
  • An info control explains: "One of Abby's specials. Counts as one of your box dishes — the upgrade is added on top."
Changed FR-12.2
How it counts and charges
  • A signature dish uses one box space, exactly like any other dish.
  • Its upgrade is charged per unit, on top of the box price.
  • Changed in v1.1: the upgrade is charged on every unit of that dish. The old base/extra wording is gone — there is only one kind of unit now.
  • The upgrade is independent of personalisation. Both can apply to the same unit.

FR-13 Price calculation Back end owns the truth

Changed FR-13.1
The box size is a hard ceiling
v1.0's base/extra split is deleted. There is no boundary to split across. Total units can never exceed the box size, so every unit is priced identically.
  • Total units across all lines must always be equal to or less than the box size.
  • The back end rejects any write that would breach it, whatever the route.
  • The only way to hold more dishes is to grow the box first (FR-9), which is a separate, explicit, priced decision.
Changed FR-13.2
Apply the three parts
  • Personalisation surcharge applies per unit, across every line.
  • Signature upgrade applies per unit.
  • Add both to the box price, as shown in section 3.3.
  • The £15 per-dish figure now appears in exactly one place: the cost of adding a space when growing the box. It is never a line-item charge on a dish.
FR-13.3
Front end estimates, back end decides
  • The front end may calculate the total for instant feedback. It is labelled "Estimated total".
  • The back end must recalculate independently at checkout and is the authority.
  • If the two disagree, show the back-end figure and tell the customer the price has been updated.
  • Never let a client-supplied price reach the payment step.

FR-14 Continue Front end

FR-14.1
Continue is gated on a full box
  • The button is disabled until base units equal the box size.
  • While disabled, it reads "Add N more dishes" (singular "dish" when N is 1).
  • When enabled, it reads "Continue to extras" and shows a forward arrow.
  • A "Secure checkout" reassurance line sits under the label.
FR-14.2
On continue
  • Persist the current box to the server, then navigate to Step 3.
  • If persisting fails, stay on the page and show a recoverable error. Do not lose the selection.

FR-15 Mobile behaviour Front end

FR-15.1
Layout changes
  • The two-column layout becomes a single column at 860px and below.
  • The box panel becomes a bottom bar that can be expanded into a sheet.
  • The bar shows the short label "Continue" or "Add N more".
  • The filter panel becomes a full-screen sheet with its own header and apply button.
  • The dish modal becomes full screen with a back control.
FR-15.2
Touch instead of hover
  • Hover tooltips are hidden on touch devices.
  • The same information is available through a tappable info control that opens an inline panel.

FR-16 Help panel Front end Back end

FR-16.1
FAQ

  • Shows the 4 most popular questions first, with "View all questions" to reveal the rest.
  • A search field filters questions and answers by text and shows "Showing N results for '...'".
  • Each question expands and collapses independently.
  • Show a helpful message when a search returns nothing.
FR-16.2

  • Fields: name, email, question. All three are required.
  • The send button stays disabled until the name and question are not empty and the email looks valid.
  • After sending, show a confirmation and offer to ask another question.
  • The back end must send the message and record it. Do not rely on a client-only success state.
FR-16.3

FR-17 Messages and feedback Front end

FR-17.1

  • A new message replaces the current one and restarts the timer.
  • Messages describe what happened using the same words as the button that caused it.
FR-17.2

FR-18 Accessibility Front end

FR-18.1

  • Quantity steppers are grouped and labelled "Quantity".

Added: keywords, optionGroups, and nutritionVariants. Removed: the assumption that nutrition is a single per-225g block that the front end scales.

  longDesc:    "Succulent prawns and crab in a rich, savoury okra stew...",

6.4 Options catalogue (back end owns this) Changed

Restructured from four named lists into a generic ordered list of groups, so a group can be renamed, repriced, reordered, added or retired without a front-end change. Heat is now a priced group like any other.

{
  groups: [
    { key:"portion", label:"Portion", select:"one", order:1,
      helpText:"How much food arrives in each container.",
      options: [
        { key:"light", label:"Light table", note:"225g", price:0,    abbyChoice:true },
        { key:"full",  label:"Full table",  note:"450g", price:1000 } ] },

    { key:"protein", label:"Protein", select:"one", order:2,
      options: [
        { key:"prawns",  label:"King prawns", price:0 },
        { key:"chicken", label:"Chicken",     price:0,   abbyChoice:true },
        { key:"salmon",  label:"Salmon",      price:300 },
        { key:"mixed",   label:"Mixed meats", price:400 } ] },

    { key:"side", label:"Side", select:"one", order:3,
      options: [
        { key:"none",     label:"No side",   price:0 },
        { key:"wildrice", label:"Wild rice", price:200, abbyChoice:true },
        { key:"quinoa",   label:"Quinoa",    price:200 },
        { key:"plantain", label:"Plantain",  price:200 } ] },

    { key:"heat", label:"Heat", select:"one", order:4,
      options: [                          // priced like any other group
        { key:"none",   label:"None",   price:0 },
        { key:"low",    label:"Low",    price:0 },
        { key:"medium", label:"Medium", price:0, abbyChoice:true },
        { key:"high",   label:"High",   price:0 } ] }
  ]
}

A dish narrows this catalogue through its own optionGroups (§6.1). The catalogue says what could exist; the dish says what applies.

6.5 Configuration (back end owns this) Changed

Removed perExtraDish as a line-item charge and renamed it to what it now is: the cost of one extra space when growing the box. maxSize is a tunable value, not a ceiling baked into the design. Added the back-to-top trigger and the results page size.

{
  minSize:            6,
  maxSize:            30,      // tunable — the front end must never hard-code this
  presets:            { "6":9500, "12":17000, "18":24000 },   // pence
  pricePerAddedSpace: 1500,    // cost of one more space when growing the box
  formulaBase:        9500,    // price of the minimum box
  referencePerDish:   1800,    // used only in comparison copy
  deliveryList:       1000,
  deliveryCharged:    0,
  earliestDelivery:   "2026-08-06",
  resultsPageSize:    8,
  backToTopTrigger:   { type:"cardIndex", value:10 }   // or { type:"pixels", value:1400 }
}
Money is stored in pence as whole numbers. Never use floating point for currency. Format to pounds only at the point of display.

7. Back-end API

MethodPathPurpose

POST/api/box/{boxId}/linesAdd a unit of a dish with a personalisation

DELETE/api/box/{boxId}/lines/{lineId}Remove a line
POST/api/box/{boxId}/quoteReturn the authoritative price breakdown
POST/api/box/{boxId}/continueValidate the box is full and advance to Step 3
GET/api/help/faqFAQ list with optional search
POST/api/help/enquirySend a question by email

Every write returns the whole box plus the quote

This keeps the front end and back end from drifting apart. One round trip, one source of truth.

{
  box: { boxId, size, price, lines: [...] },
  quote: {
    boxPrice:          9500,
    personalisation:   1200,
    signatureUpgrades: 900,
    deliveryList:      1000,
    deliveryCharged:   0,
    total:             11600,
    unitsSelected:     6,
    boxSize:           6,
    spacesLeft:        0,
    isFull:            true
  }
}

Changed in v1.1: extraDishes, extraPersonalisation, baseUnits and extraUnits are gone. unitsSelected can never exceed boxSize.

Rules the back end must enforce Changed

  • Reject a box size below the configured minimum or above the configured maximum.
  • Reject a box size smaller than the number of units already selected.
  • Reject any write that would take total units above the box size. This is the rule that replaces extras — there is no overflow path.
  • Reject any option key that is not in the current catalogue.
  • Reject any option key the dish does not offer, even when it exists in the catalogue (FR-11.5).
  • Reject a personalisation missing a required group, or with no option selected in a required group.
  • Reject adding a dish that is unavailable.
  • Recalculate the price from scratch on every write. Never trust a price sent by the client.
  • Merge a new unit into a matching line rather than creating a duplicate.
  • Refuse "continue" unless total units equal the box size.

10. Out of scope

  • Step 1 (choose box), Step 3 (extras), Step 4 (review) and Step 5 (checkout). Only the links into and out of Step 2 belong here.
  • Payment, addresses, delivery-slot selection and account creation.
  • Gifting. Gifting lives only on the Gifting page and never appears as a toggle or add-on in this flow.
  • The curated-box path (Personalise → Add-ons → Checkout). That is a separate, deliberately distinct journey.
  • Menu content management and photography pipeline.
  • Subscription or repeat-order logic.

Reverse-engineered from the Step 2 prototype. Where the prototype held values in the client (box prices, option surcharges, the earliest delivery date, the FAQ list), those values are listed here as back-end configuration. The prototype's numbers are the correct starting values, but the production front end must read them from the API rather than hold its own copy.
